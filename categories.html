    Very Small Size Soccer


    Very Small Size Soccer (VSSS) is a competition that requires development in the RobôCIn’s main areas, computer vision to detect information about the game situation; artificial intelligence to plan all robots movements, so the team can score goals, and defend ourselves from opponents; and robotics that involve electronics and mechanics to make agents that needs to move perfectly on the field. Together with computer vision and A.I. makes our system of robots that play soccer.VSS also demands small players (robots), so teams need to build a consistent robotics project with limited space. Another important feature about the systems is the real-time response, so RobôCIn develops an efficient computer vision and A.I., otherwise, the robots won't react properly in the game.
    RobôCIn began its research in this category in 2016.

    Small Size League


    A Small Size robot soccer game takes place between two teams of six robots each. Each robot must conform to the dimensions as specified in the F180 rules: the robot must fit within an 180 mm diameter circle and must be no higher than 15 cm. The robots play soccer with an orange golf ball on a green carpeted field that is 9 m long by 6 m wide. - See more at: https://www.robocup.org/leagues/7#sthash.3f3g8oI0.dpuf
    Retrieved from : https://www.robocup.org/leagues/7.
    We started our research in this category in 2018, and that same year we participated in the Latin American Robotics Competition.
